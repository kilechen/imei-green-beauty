Who We Are
Three Generations of Beauty, One Mission
IMEI Cosmetics was founded over 70 years ago when our grandfather, a pharmacist apprentice, saw Taiwan's dependence on imported beauty products and decided to change it. He created one of Taiwan's first homegrown cosmetics brands. Three generations later, under CEO Wayne Chen, we've evolved from a pure OEM factory into a sustainability-focused ODM/OBM manufacturer specializing in skincare, sun care, base makeup, and personal care.
Today, our mission is clear: combine decades of formulation expertise with green innovation to build a more sustainable beauty industry. We believe cosmetics manufacturing should leave a smaller footprint while delivering bigger results.

1950s — The Beginning

Our grandfather, a pharmacist apprentice, began developing and manufacturing cosmetics in Taiwan, eventually creating the iconic "Mei Shuang Shuang" brand with over 600 retail locations nationwide.

1980s-90s — Global Expansion

Under second-generation leadership, IMEI secured OEM contracts with internationally recognized beauty brands and expanded into export markets across Asia.

2010s — Transformation

Wayne Chen, with a background in PwC's finance practice, took over and modernized operations: ERP systems, digital workflows, and a strategic pivot from pure manufacturing to full-service ODM.

2020s — Green Sustainability

Launched GreenAct Lab, partnered with Taiwan's ITRI for pineapple-leaf ingredient development, deployed the Smart Circular Box system, and began carbon inventory tracking and renewable energy adoption.

Our Direction

Sustainable Ingredients
Pineapple Leaf Extract: From Farm Waste to Skincare
In collaboration with Taiwan's Industrial Technology Research Institute (ITRI), we developed a process to extract bioactive compounds from pineapple leaves, an agricultural by-product that is typically discarded or burned after harvest. This upcycled ingredient is now used in our cleansers and skincare formulations.

Turning Agricultural Waste into Beauty Innovation

Taiwan produces over 400,000 tons of pineapple annually. After harvest, the leaves are usually burned or left to decompose, contributing to air pollution and wasted biomass. Our extraction technology captures the beneficial compounds from these leaves, creating a truly circular ingredient story that brands can proudly tell their customers.

Supported by Taiwan's Ministry of Economic Affairs subsidy program for "Pineapple By-product Skincare Ingredient Pilot Production."

Upcycled Source Pineapple leaves from Taiwanese farms, otherwise discarded
ITRI Partnership Joint R&D with Taiwan's top government research institute
Product Applications Facial cleansers, serums, and skincare formulations

Circular Economy in Action
The Smart Circular Box: Rethinking Beauty Logistics
The beauty industry's supply chain runs on single-use cardboard and plastic packaging. Every shipment between raw material suppliers, manufacturers, and brand owners generates waste that ends up in landfills. Our Smart Circular Box is a reusable, IoT-enabled shipping container designed specifically for the cosmetics supply chain.
Supplier Ships raw materials
→
IMEI Factory Manufactures products
→
Brand / Warehouse Receives finished goods
→
Box Returns Cleaned & reused
📡

IoT & RFID Tracking

Each box has digital sensor tags that record shipping timestamps, product information, and packaging flow. Full traceability for ESG reporting.

♻️

Closed-Loop Reuse

Boxes are collected, cleaned, sorted, and put back into circulation. Eliminates single-use cardboard waste between supply chain partners.

📊

Data-Driven Optimization

AI analytics optimize packaging recovery rates and logistics scheduling. Future capability: carbon emission tracking per shipment.

🏷️

For Brand Owners

Digital tracking records provide a verified green provenance story for consumers. Reduced packaging costs. A tangible sustainability initiative to include in corporate ESG reports and marketing communications.

🏭

For Suppliers & Logistics

Precise inventory management of packaging quantities and usage. Reduced damage and warehouse pressure. Lower operational costs from eliminating disposable shipping materials and printed labels.
